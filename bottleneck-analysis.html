Lead Doc

Three Bottlenecks I Found Inside Your Funnel - John Eringman

All three install in less than 7 days from kickoff. Built to compound. Order by impact.

Snapshot

  • 1.3M TikTok + 164K IG @johnefinance + YouTube @johnefinance + CNBC / MarketWatch / FinCon press
  • Finance grad, University of Cincinnati - credentialed educator, not a self-taught influencer
  • Bio: "no sales pitches, no get rich quick schemes, no gimmicks" - the strongest skilled-no-offer signal in the v4 D100 list
  • Zero owned monetisation layer across the entire 1.3M+ audience
  • No email list visible, no lead magnet, no paid product
  • All income is sponsorship-dependent - platform-owned, platform-controlled

Bottleneck #1 - 1.3M followers with zero owned audience - one algorithm change ends the income

The asymmetry: John has built one of the most trusted personal finance audiences on TikTok. 1.3M followers who followed him specifically because he does NOT pitch. That trust type is rare and valuable. But the entire relationship lives on a platform he does not control. TikTok has already been threatened with US bans twice. Instagram algorithms punish certain content categories. One policy change, one account flag, one algorithm shift - and the 1.3M is unreachable overnight. There is no email list to fall back on. There is no owned channel.

A "Personal Finance Health Check" scorecard - 5 questions, returns a score out of 100 with a personalised action plan, captures email without asking for money - converts the casual follower into an owned subscriber John can reach regardless of what TikTok or Instagram does next.

Why it's costing him: - 1.3M TikTok + 164K IG with a 5-10% opt-in rate on the right lead magnet = 65,000-143,000 email subscribers from the existing audience alone - Without the email list, every other monetisation layer (newsletter, cohort, sponsorship alternatives) is impossible at scale - Platform dependency is not a "risk to think about later" - it is an active income ceiling happening right now - CNBC / MarketWatch placed him alongside financial journalists. His audience expects an editorial-grade product. The scorecard is the entry point.

The fix: - "Personal Finance Health Check" - 5-question quiz on ScoreApp or Typeform - Questions: income vs expenses ratio, debt status, emergency fund coverage, investment activity, financial goal clarity - Returns score 0-100 with a tier-specific 3-step action plan (PDF, clean, no affiliate links) - Captures email, tags by score tier, triggers the welcome sequence - Bio link routes to scorecard as the primary CTA across TikTok, IG, and YouTube

Estimated impact: Year-1 floor $60-180K/yr from email-driven newsletter subscriptions + cohort sales once the list exists. Compounds for the life of the list - which, unlike the TikTok following, he owns.

Why this is #1: The email list is the infrastructure every other bottleneck depends on. Without it, the newsletter has no distribution and the cohort has no pre-warm audience. The scorecard is the fastest install of the three.

Bottleneck #2 - Finance grad with CNBC coverage has no paid newsletter - the credibility gap

The leak: John has CNBC + MarketWatch + FinCon press placements. That level of press coverage signals an editorial standard that a paid newsletter would credibly match. The audience he has built is specifically looking for honest, no-pitch financial education - the type they currently cannot find inside the sponsored-content economy of TikTok. The people who follow John because his bio says "no sales pitches" are the exact audience for a paid newsletter where the product promise is "no sponsored content, no affiliate links, just the real numbers."

A paid newsletter at $9-$15/month positions as the credibility-aligned alternative to algorithm-dependent content - John's existing brand promise, turned into a product.

Why it's costing him: - 1,000 paid newsletter subscribers at $9/month = $108K/yr ARR. That number is a floor, not a ceiling, for someone with 1.3M TikTok + press coverage - Sponsorship income requires constant content output and is at brand-deal mercy. A newsletter subscription is recurring, predictable, and publisher-controlled - The "no sales pitches" brand is a competitive advantage for a paid newsletter - it is the pitch. Other personal finance creators monetise through affiliate deals and course launches. John's newsletter earns by being the alternative. - Every month without a paid newsletter = another month of the trust asset depreciating into free content with no economic conversion

The fix: - "The Real Numbers" paid newsletter - $9/month or $79/year (annual saves ~25%) - Weekly edition: one finance concept, one real data set, one specific action step - No affiliate links inside the newsletter. No sponsored content inside the newsletter. The purity of the product is the product. - Substack or Ghost (Ghost preferred for design control + no platform risk) - Free tier: 1 issue/month + archive. Paid tier: weekly issue + full archive + monthly Q&A thread

Estimated impact: Year-1 floor $100-300K/yr ARR at 1,000-3,000 paid subscribers. Scales with list growth from Bottleneck #1. Compounds month-over-month because the subscription is recurring.

Why this is #2: The newsletter is the highest-leverage monetisation that matches the "honest education" brand without compromising it. It is the credibility-aligned income layer that does not require John to "sell" anything - it is simply paid access to what he already produces. Depends on Bottleneck #1 for distribution scale, but can launch to the existing IG/TikTok audience immediately.

Bottleneck #3 - Finance degree sits unused - the credentialed cohort no one is building

The contradiction: John has a University of Cincinnati Finance degree. He has CNBC and MarketWatch press placements. He has 1.3M people who follow him because he understands money. None of that credentialing is being used to justify a premium educational product. A $297-$497 cohort built around his Finance-grad framework is not a "course" - it is a credentialed instructor teaching in a live classroom environment. That is a fundamentally different positioning from the average TikTok finance creator selling a PDF.

A "Financial Independence Cohort" - 8-week live programme, 20-30 students per cohort, John as the credentialed instructor - is the one offer that cannot be replicated by anyone without his specific background.

Why it's costing him: - A single cohort of 30 students at $297 = $8,910. Two cohorts per year = $17,820. That is a floor, not a ceiling - price scales with demand and testimonials - The cohort positions John as a teacher, not a content creator. That distinction matters for the "no sales pitches" audience - they will pay $297 to learn from a Finance grad they already trust - Testimonials from a cohort feed the newsletter and the scorecard - social proof compounds - The cohort also solves the "I want more than content" problem that John's most engaged followers have. Right now there is nowhere for the person who wants to go deeper. The cohort is that place.

The fix: - "Financial Independence Cohort" - 8-week live programme, $297 standard / $497 with 1:1 review session - Weeks 1-2: foundation (budget, debt map, emergency fund) - Weeks 3-4: debt elimination framework - Weeks 5-6: investing basics for people who've never invested - Weeks 7-8: financial independence path + personalised 12-month plan - Live weekly call, recordings available, private community for cohort members - Intake: application + email list (feeds from Bottleneck #1) - Caps at 30 per cohort to preserve the direct-access feel

Estimated impact: Year-1 floor $35-120K/yr at 2-4 cohorts x 20-40 students x $297-$497. Scales with list growth and testimonials. Each cohort generates 20-40 testimonials that fuel the newsletter and paid traffic.

Why this is #3: Smaller Year-1 ceiling than the newsletter but produces the testimonials and social proof that make everything else compound faster. Requires the email list (Bottleneck #1) and ideally some newsletter subscribers (Bottleneck #2) as the warm audience before launch.
