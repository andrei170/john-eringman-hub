Foundation

Copy Strategy - John Eringman

Foundation doc · Voice, brand pillars, headline bank, framing AG1 will write to.

Voice

Clear, measured, credentialed. John's voice is the antithesis of personal finance hype. He does not project excitement about money. He presents numbers, draws clear conclusions, and respects the reader's intelligence. Finance grad register: precise without being academic. Never condescending. Always specific - not "you could save more" but "here is the number and here is why."

Use: real numbers, specific, honest, clear, framework, calculation, your money, your situation, what this means for you, the actual math, in plain terms Avoid: game-changer, wealth mindset, financial freedom (unless followed by a specific number), passive income (as a promise), abundance, get rich, level up, no more financial stress (unless followed by a mechanism), gimmicks (banned by his own bio), hustle culture framing

Brand Pillars

  1. Honest education - the core brand promise. Copy must never oversell. Every claim has a number or a mechanism behind it. "You could earn $10,000 more" requires the mechanism. "Here's how much debt you'd have paid off" shows the calculation.
  2. No hype - the "no sales pitches, no get rich quick schemes, no gimmicks" bio is the brand in three clauses. Every piece of copy passes the bio test: would John's bio reject this line?
  3. Credentialed clarity - Finance degree + CNBC/MarketWatch press. Copy should feel like a well-written financial explainer from a publication John would be quoted in. Not a pitch. Not a testimonial reel.
  4. Platform-independent thinking - John teaches people to own their financial future. The newsletter and cohort are the product equivalent of that: income he owns, an audience he owns, education that is not at platform mercy.

Headline Bank

For the Financial Independence Cohort landing page, hub callouts, and ad creative:

  • Personal finance education without the pitch.
  • The real numbers. No affiliate links. No sponsored content.
  • A Finance grad who teaches what he was taught. Not what sells.
  • Your financial health score. Five questions. One honest action plan.
  • 1.3 million people trust John not to pitch. Here is what he built for the ones who want to go deeper.
  • No get-rich-quick. No gimmicks. Just the math and what it means for you.
  • The paid newsletter that earns by being the alternative to sponsored content.
  • Eight weeks. A credentialed instructor. Your 12-month financial independence plan.

Framing for the Funnel

  • Top of funnel (scorecard, short-form content): diagnostic-led, not pitch-led. "What is your financial health score?" not "Join thousands of people who fixed their finances." Specificity and honesty beat social proof at this stage for John's audience.
  • Mid funnel (newsletter landing, cohort landing): credibility-led. Finance degree + CNBC + MarketWatch. The product is the same honest analysis, in a format John controls, with no platform in between.
  • High funnel (post-cohort, long-term): referral-led. Cohort graduates who achieved specific outcomes become the social proof that feeds the next cohort intake. Their testimonials are specific and numerical ("paid off $14K in 8 weeks"), not vague ("changed my life").

Banned Phrases

Universal AG1 ban list, plus John-specific:

  • Nothing from his own bio turned into a selling point without attribution ("no sales pitches" can be referenced, not used as hype)
  • No "wealth mindset" or "financial freedom" without a specific number
  • No "join the community" without a specific community benefit
  • No countdown timers or fake scarcity in any copy
  • No "limited spots" unless it is literally true (cohort caps are real, so this is allowed)
  • No affiliate link references in the paid newsletter copy or landing page

Tone Calibration By Surface

Surface Register Length
TikTok / IG hook Data-led, one specific number or observation 1 line
Scorecard CTA Diagnostic, curious, no pressure 1 sentence
Newsletter landing Editorial, precise, CNBC-adjacent register 3-4 short paragraphs
Cohort landing hero Credentialed instructor framing, specific outcomes 1 promise + 1 mechanism
Email subject Clear + specific, no clickbait 5-8 words
FAQ Plain, direct, no legal hedge 2-3 sentences each

Voice Test

Every paragraph passes if it could appear in a MarketWatch feature or a well-written Substack from a finance journalist John would respect. If it sounds like a course-bro, rewrite it. If it sounds like a sponsored post, delete it.
