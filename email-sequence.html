Phase 1+2

Email Sequences - John Eringman

Phase 1 + 2 deliverable · 8-touch welcome flow, pre-cohort nurture (3 touches), post-cohort follow-up (4 touches over 14 days).

Sequence 1 - Welcome Flow (10 days, 8 touches)

Triggered when a new subscriber completes the Personal Finance Health Check. Segmented by score tier (0-40 / 41-70 / 71-100).

T+0 - Immediate (Score Result)

Subject: Your financial health score - [X/100] + what it means Body: Score reveal, 2-sentence context on what the score means for their specific tier, 3-step action plan personalised by tier, link to download the PDF version, one sentence about The Real Numbers newsletter as the ongoing education layer (no ask, just a mention). Goal: deliver the promise immediately. Set tone as precise and honest.

T+1 day - The Real Numbers

Subject: Why I don't do affiliate links (and what I do instead) Body: Short post on why John built the newsletter without sponsored content or affiliate links. The newsletter as the product, not the pitch. Closes with: "If that sounds like the kind of finance content you've been looking for, the first issue is free. Link below." Goal: introduce the paid newsletter as a credibility-aligned extension of the scorecard, not a sales email.

T+3 days - The Debt Math

Subject: The number most people with debt never calculate Body: Specific calculation - what a $15,000 credit card balance at 22% APR actually costs over 5 years of minimum payments. Clear numbers, no judgment. CTA: "I break down this kind of math every week in The Real Numbers. Issue 1 is free." Goal: demonstrate the value of the newsletter by giving a sample of the analytical depth it provides.

T+5 days - The Investing Gap

Subject: What $200/month looks like at 65 (the honest version) Body: Real compound interest table for $200/month over 30 years at 7% average annual return. Most people have seen this stat but have never seen it calculated against their own timeline. Specific and personal. CTA: "Your score showed [gap]. Here is how the investing section of the Financial Independence Cohort addresses it." Goal: introduce the cohort as the live, interactive version of the scorecard education.

T+7 days - The Platform Risk Story

Subject: What happens when your financial plan depends on one source Body: Frame the TikTok ban story through a personal finance lens - just like income that depends on a single source of income is fragile, a financial plan built on one variable is fragile. Pivot to the diversification principle. The cohort teaches this. CTA: "Applications for the next cohort open in [X days]. Link below." Goal: prime the cohort application without a hard sell. The connection to their financial situation makes it feel relevant, not promotional.

T+9 days - Soft Pitch Open

Subject: Quick question before I move on Body: Two-line check-in. "Did the scorecard plan feel accurate for where you're at? I'm curious what landed and what didn't." Link to the newsletter free issue + cohort application. "Hit reply if you want to ask anything directly. I read every one." Goal: segment hot vs warm. Hot replies get a personal response. Warm gets routed to the newsletter CTA.

T+10 days - Last Touch (segmented)

  • Score 71-100 (Investment-ready): "Your score suggests the cohort's investing and financial independence modules are where you'd get the most value. Applications are open." Routes to cohort application page.
  • Score 41-70 (Foundations pool): Final newsletter CTA. "The Real Numbers runs every week. Issue 1 is free - no card, no commitment." Routes to newsletter landing.
  • Score 0-40 (Debt / basics): "The foundational modules of the cohort are built specifically for where your score landed. Start there." Routes to cohort application with a "debt-priority" tag pre-populated.

Sequence 2 - Pre-Cohort Nurture (3 touches before first session)

Triggered when someone applies and is accepted into the Financial Independence Cohort.

T-7 days

Subject: Before we start - three questions I'll ask in week one Body: The three orientation questions John will ask in the first live session. Helps the cohort member arrive prepared with real numbers: current monthly income, total debt balance, current savings rate percentage. Frames the cohort as a working session, not a lecture.

T-3 days

Subject: What to have ready for our first call Body: Practical prep list: income statement (last 3 months), current debt balances, last 3 months of bank statements (or a spending estimate). One story about why real numbers matter more than estimates. Sets the expectation that this is a working cohort.

T-1 day

Subject: Tomorrow at [time] - link inside + one thing to bring Body: Zoom link, calendar hold reminder, and one specific number to have ready: "What is your current monthly take-home after tax?" That is the first number we will use in session one.

Sequence 3 - Post-Cohort Follow-Up (4 touches over 14 days)

Triggered after the 8-week cohort completes.

T+1 hour - Completion + Recap

Subject: Week 8 done - what you built over 8 weeks Body: Short summary of the key frameworks covered, the subscriber's personalised 12-month plan (reference their specific numbers from week 1 intake), links to all session recordings. Closes with: "The cohort community stays open for 90 days post-completion."

T+3 days - The Specific Resource

Subject: The [framework] I mentioned in week 6 Body: Whatever specific resource was most referenced in that cohort - the debt elimination calculator, the emergency fund sizing guide, the investment starting checklist. Delivered clean, no upsell.

T+7 days - The Check-In

Subject: Quick - have you run the 12-month plan yet? Body: Single accountability nudge. "Run the plan against your actual numbers from week 8. Reply with one number: where do you end up at month 12 if you follow it?" One-line replies are enough. Creates engagement and real data for testimonials.

T+14 days - The Continuation

Subject: What's next after the cohort Body: Two paths offered: (1) continue with The Real Numbers newsletter for ongoing education without another cohort commitment, (2) refer someone who would benefit from the next cohort and get a free quarter of the paid newsletter. No hard sell. Two clean options.

Subject Line Bank (rotation)

For ongoing newsletter broadcasts beyond the welcome sequence:

  • "The compound interest math everyone gets wrong"
  • "Your credit score vs your credit worthiness"
  • "Three numbers I'd run before any major financial decision"
  • "What 10 years of minimum payments actually costs"
  • "The savings rate that changes the math"
  • "What CNBC won't tell you about personal finance content"
  • "No sponsors. No affiliate links. Just this week's numbers."

Triggers + Tags

  • Tag by score tier on quiz completion (finance-score-high / finance-score-mid / finance-score-low)
  • Tag by purchase (newsletter-paid / cohort-enrolled / cohort-graduate)
  • Tag by content engagement (opened 5+ in 30 days = "warm subscriber")
  • Auto-suppress promotional emails during cohort weeks (replace with cohort-specific track)
  • Re-engagement campaign at 45 days of inactivity: "Your financial health score from [date] - has anything changed?"
